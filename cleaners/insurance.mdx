As a Zulma Pro you run your own independent cleaning or services business. That means you carry your own insurance for the work you do. This page explains exactly what is required, why it protects you, and how it fits with the coverage Zulma carries on top.

## What you need to carry

You carry your own **commercial general liability (CGL)** insurance of at least **$2,000,000 (CAD)**. This is the front-line coverage for the jobs you work, and it is a normal cost of running an independent cleaning or services business.

<Info>
Commercial general liability covers third-party claims like accidental property damage or injury that can happen while you are working. It is the standard policy independent contractors carry, and most insurers in Canada offer it for cleaning and home-services work.
</Info>

| What | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Coverage type | Commercial general liability (CGL) |
| Minimum amount | $2,000,000 CAD |
| Who it covers | You, for the work you perform |
| When it is confirmed | During onboarding, before you take jobs |

## Why you carry your own

You are an independent contractor, not an employee. You decide how, when, and whether you work, you can decline jobs, and you can work for others. Carrying your own policy is part of that independence. It keeps you protected directly, in your own name, for the work you choose to take on.

Your policy is yours. If you also work outside Zulma, the same coverage follows you to that work.

## Zulma's coverage sits on top

Your own policy is the front line. Zulma carries platform-level coverage above that, so there are two layers of protection in place:

| Layer | Who carries it |
|---|---|
| Your jobs | You, with your own $2M CGL |
| The platform | Zulma, with $5M commercial general liability plus bonding and WSIB |

Zulma's coverage is platform-level protection. It does not replace your own policy, and your own policy does not replace Zulma's. They work together.

## New markets and the grace period

We do not want insurance to be the thing that keeps a good Pro from getting started. When Zulma opens a **new market**, there may be a grace period to get your policy in place after you start. If that applies to your area, we communicate the timeline clearly for your market during onboarding.

<Note>
The grace period, when it exists, is specific to your market and is always communicated to you. Where there is no grace period, your $2M CGL needs to be confirmed before you take jobs. Either way, you will always know where you stand.
</Note>

## Short-term-rental turnover work

If you take on **short-term-rental turnover jobs** (Zulma for Hosts in Niagara Falls and Niagara-on-the-Lake), the requirement is the same: your own **$2,000,000 commercial general liability** policy. There is no separate or higher insurance bar for turnover work. The $2M CGL you carry for your regular jobs is what covers it.

<Tip>
Already carrying $2M CGL for your residential, lawn, or detailing work? You are set for turnover work too. One policy at $2M covers all of it.
</Tip>

## What to have ready

<Steps>
<Step title="Get a CGL policy">
Arrange commercial general liability coverage of at least $2,000,000 CAD from an insurer that covers cleaning or home-services work.
</Step>
<Step title="Confirm it during onboarding">
We confirm your insurance details as part of getting you approved. If your market has a grace period, we tell you the timeline then.
</Step>
<Step title="Keep it current">
Keep your policy active for as long as you take jobs. Renew it before it lapses so your coverage never has a gap.
</Step>
</Steps>
