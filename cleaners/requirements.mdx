Zulma is selective so that customers know they can trust every crew member who walks through their door. The requirements below apply to all applicants. Cleaning experience is a plus but is not required — read on for more detail.

## Checklist

Before you apply, confirm you meet all of the following:

- **18 years or older**
- **Legal right to work in Canada**
- **Reliable transportation** to job sites anywhere in the Niagara region
- **Smartphone** running iOS 15+ or Android 11+
- **Pass a vulnerable-sector criminal record check** (Zulma coordinates this; it's free)
- **Complete WHMIS 2015 online training** — required for anyone handling commercial cleaning chemicals; Zulma provides this free of charge
- **Carry your own $2M Commercial General Liability insurance** (typically $40–80/month)
- **Three professional references** — cleaning experience preferred but not required; Zulma actually calls them
- **Comfortable working independently in client homes**

<Note>
Cleaning experience is preferred but not required. Many of Zulma's top earners started without any prior professional cleaning background. What matters most is reliability, care, and respect in someone's home.
</Note>

## Why you need your own $2M CGL insurance

You might wonder why you need your own policy if Zulma carries a $5M umbrella. Here is the full picture.

Zulma's $5M umbrella covers **platform-level liability** and backstops crew members for catastrophic claims — those that exceed your own policy limit. However, Zulma's insurer (Zensurance) requires every independent contractor crew member to carry their own **$2M Commercial General Liability policy** as the front-line coverage for the actual work you perform.

There are two practical reasons this matters for you personally:

1. **It protects your personal assets.** Your own CGL insurer fights for you specifically — not just for Zulma — if a damage or injury claim arises from your work.
2. **It strengthens your independent contractor tax status.** Carrying your own business insurance is one of the markers the CRA looks at when determining whether someone is a genuine independent business operator. This protects your IC classification under CRA rules.

Most sole-proprietor cleaners in Ontario pay $40–80/month for a $2M CGL policy. Brokers who commonly serve this market include Zensurance, APOLLO, and ALIGNED.

<Tip>
If a damage claim ever arises, your $2M CGL responds first. If anything exceeds your limit, Zulma's $5M umbrella backstops it. Theft claims are covered separately by Zulma's janitorial bond. You will never face a claim alone.
</Tip>

## Skills check before solo jobs

Your first booking is shadowed by a senior crew member. You do not take independent jobs until you pass the skills check. This protects both you and the customer.

After that, crew must maintain a **4.7★ minimum rating** across their last 20 jobs. Falling below that threshold results in a pause for retraining or removal from the platform.
