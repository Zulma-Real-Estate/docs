## What's included

Every item below is part of the base price.

**Dust removal**
- HEPA vacuum of every surface. Drywall dust is microscopic and settles in vents, fabric, and electronics, so multiple passes are standard
- Wet-wipe of all hard surfaces, top-to-bottom
- HVAC vents and ductwork: full dust removal
- Light fixtures and ceiling fans

**Throughout the home**
- All surfaces, counters, and shelves wiped down
- Windows and sills wiped (interior glass available as an add-on)
- Floors vacuumed and mopped
- Bathrooms and kitchen detailed
- Inside cabinets and drawers

## What's not included

<Note>
  The following are out of scope for a post-construction clean.

  - Construction debris requiring a dumpster (the haul-away add-on handles smaller volumes)
  - Painting touch-ups or surface repair
  - Carpet steam cleaning (a separate specialist is recommended)
  - Hazardous materials such as asbestos or lead paint
</Note>

## When to choose a post-construction clean

**Just-renovated kitchens or bathrooms.** Drywall dust gets everywhere. This clean handles the deep dust removal so the room is actually usable.

**Whole-home renovations.** Larger projects often need a multi-cleaner team. Book early and Zulma will allocate the crew needed for a single-day completion.

**New construction handover.** Builders often leave a basic broom-clean. This service gets the home truly move-in ready before unpacking.

<Tip>
  Smaller renovation, like a single-room repaint? A [Deep Clean](/services/deep-clean) with the baseboards and HVAC add-ons may be enough, and it costs less. Tell Zulma what you've done and they'll recommend the right tier.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When should I book, right after the contractor leaves?">
    Wait 24 hours so airborne dust has fully settled, then book. Cleaning too early just stirs the dust back up. If your renovation is staged, book a clean after each phase.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is post-construction cleaning more expensive than a deep clean?">
    It takes longer (6–8 hours vs 4–6) and requires HEPA-filtered equipment plus more aggressive techniques. Drywall dust is fine and gets into HVAC vents, fabric, and electronics, so it takes multiple passes to fully remove.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can you remove construction debris?">
    Smaller volumes, yes, with the $120 haul-away add-on. For dumpster-loads of debris, a junk-removal service is recommended. Zulma can refer one in your area.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will you handle paint or stain removal?">
    Light overspray and splatters on hard surfaces, yes. For drips or larger areas, a painter's touch-up is the safer answer. Aggressive treatment risks damaging new finishes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to be home?">
    Not necessarily, but for post-construction Zulma recommends a brief walkthrough at the start so the Pro understands which surfaces are sensitive: custom finishes, just-installed flooring, and similar.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
